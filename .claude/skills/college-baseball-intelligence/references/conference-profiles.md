# Conference Profiles

## Tier 1: SEC
16 teams. Deepest conference. 6-8 tournament-level programs. .500 SEC record often earns at-large.
~25% of CWS appearances last decade. Net importer conference (portal + national recruiting).
Powers: LSU, Vanderbilt, Arkansas, Florida, Tennessee, Ole Miss, Texas A&M, Texas

## Tier 2: ACC, Big 12, Big Ten
ACC (18 teams): Elite top-end, softer middle. Premier pitching prospect factory.
Powers: Florida State, Wake Forest, Clemson, NC State, Virginia, Louisville

Big 12 (16 teams): Offense-heavy. Hitter-friendly parks inflate stats 5-10%.
Powers: TCU, Oklahoma State, Texas Tech

Big Ten (18 teams): Weather-affected early season. February stats especially unreliable.
Powers: Michigan, Indiana, Oregon, UCLA

## Tier 3: Sun Belt, AAC, CUSA, WCC
Sun Belt: Strongest mid-major. Multiple regional hosts. Treat with full rigor.
Powers: Southern Miss, Louisiana, Coastal Carolina

AAC: East Carolina anchor. If ECU is strong, AAC sends 2-3 teams.
Powers: East Carolina, Tulane, Wichita State

CUSA: Liberty carries the flag. Top team often hosts.
WCC: Gonzaga-anchored. Often nationally undervalued.

## Tier 4: Colonial, MVC, Southern, A-10
Mid-major with breakout potential. Conference champions are dangerous regional 4-seeds.
Watch: Campbell (Colonial), Dallas Baptist (MVC), Samford (Southern), VCU (A-10)

## Tier 5: Everything Else — BSI Territory
MEAC, SWAC, Big South, Horizon, Summit, Patriot, Ivy, NEC, MAAC, OVC, Southland, WAC, Big West, Mountain West
Deepest coverage gap in college sports. BSI mandate: same metric rigor as Tier 1.

## Conference-Adjusted Protocol
1. Pull Conference Power Index
2. Note avgWinPct and avgRunDiff per conference
3. Adjust: .320 wOBA in SEC > .340 wOBA in Big South
4. Use wRC+ and ERA- (already adjusted) as primary cross-conference metrics
5. Never compare raw ERA across conferences without context
