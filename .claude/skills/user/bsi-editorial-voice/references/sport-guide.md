## MLB

### Coverage Philosophy
Baseball rewards patience—in analysis, not just at the plate. The 162-game sample size means early-season panic is usually wrong. Focus on process over results until sample sizes matter (typically 150+ PA, 50+ IP).

### Key Metrics to Reference
- **Hitters:** wRC+, xwOBA, BB%, K%, barrel rate, sprint speed
- **Pitchers:** FIP, xERA, stuff+ (when available), K-BB%, GB%
- **Team:** run differential, BaseRuns expected record, bullpen leverage index

### BSI Differentiator
Cover the **unsexy stories** ESPN ignores—middle relievers who quietly dominate, backup catchers with elite framing, the A's and Rays doing more with less. The game isn't just Yankees-Dodgers.

### Common Pitfalls
- Overreacting to April stats
- Ignoring park factors (Coors, etc.)
- Treating pitcher wins as meaningful
- Conflating "clutch" with small sample noise

## NFL

### Coverage Philosophy
Football is a weekly sport—context changes fast. Injury reports, matchup specifics, and coaching tendencies matter more week-to-week than cumulative stats. Film matters; watching the game tells you more than the box score.

### Key Metrics to Reference
- **Offense:** EPA/play, success rate, DVOA (when citing Football Outsiders)
- **QB:** EPA/dropback, CPOE, pressure rate, time to throw
- **Defense:** pressure rate, coverage grades (PFF), run defense efficiency
- **Special teams:** Don't ignore them—they decide close games

### BSI Differentiator
**Process over results.** A team can win ugly on luck; a team can lose despite playing well. Identify which is happening before the market corrects. Also: Tennessee coverage matters here—Titans get short shrift nationally.

### Common Pitfalls
- QB wins as a stat
- Ignoring game script (garbage time points)
- Treating preseason like signal
- Narrative over evidence ("playoff experience" without specifics)

## NCAA Football

### Coverage Philosophy
The sport with the most coverage disparity. SEC and Big Ten get saturated attention; everyone else is an afterthought. BSI exists to cover the **whole sport**—including the stories ESPN ignores because they don't move TV ratings.

### Key Metrics to Reference
- **Team:** SP+ (Bill Connelly), FPI, FEI
- **Offense:** success rate, explosiveness, EPA/play
- **Defense:** havoc rate, stuff rate, passing downs success rate
- **Recruiting:** composite rankings (247), transfer portal impact

### BSI Differentiator
**Portal coverage.** The transfer portal reshaped the sport—cover the movement, the implications, the market inefficiencies. Also: Texas gets covered, but cover it *honestly*, not through burnt orange glasses.

### Common Pitfalls
- Treating recruiting rankings as destiny
- Ignoring schedule strength
- Hot takes after Week 2
- Pretending playoff expansion doesn't change incentives

## NBA

### Coverage Philosophy
The NBA is both a superstar league and a team sport—hold both truths. Stars decide series, but depth decides regular season. Load management, injury risk, and playoff seeding games make context essential.

### Key Metrics to Reference
- **Players:** PER, BPM, WS/48, TS%, usage rate
- **Team:** net rating, pace, clutch performance (with sample caveats)
- **Advanced:** RAPTOR, EPM, tracking data when available

### BSI Differentiator
The **Memphis Grizzlies** matter. Small-market teams with good stories deserve coverage beyond "will they trade everyone?" narratives. Also: the G League and development pipelines are underrated stories.

### Common Pitfalls
- Ring-chasing narratives over team building
- Ignoring rest patterns and schedule spots
- Treating January like April
- Player comparisons without positional/era context

## College Baseball

### Coverage Philosophy
**The most underserved market in sports media.** This is BSI's whitespace opportunity. No one covers college baseball with the depth it deserves—the stories, the draft implications, the regional programs building something real.

### Key Metrics to Reference
- **Hitters:** OPS, wOBA, K%, BB%, quality of contact when available
- **Pitchers:** K/9, BB/9, FIP, stuff grades (limited but growing)
- **Team:** RPI, strength of schedule, regional host potential
- **Draft:** BA/MLB Pipeline rankings, signability, college vs. pro risk

### BSI Differentiator
This is **home turf**. Austin's playing background, coaching connections (Danny Graves, Jason Marshall, Rocky Thompson), and genuine love for the game means BSI can go deeper than anyone. Cover:
- Regional programs outside the SEC/ACC duopoly
- Draft-eligible sophomores and their decisions
- JUCO-to-D1 pathways
- Summer ball as a development indicator

### Common Pitfalls
- Treating RPI like gospel (it's flawed)
- Ignoring weather/ballpark factors
- Projecting college success to pro success without adjustment
- Missing the human stories—these are 19-22 year olds navigating real decisions

## College Basketball

### Coverage Philosophy
The tournament drives attention, but the regular season reveals the teams. College basketball's long conference slates expose weaknesses that early-season neutral-site games mask. The sport's parity is its strength — any given night, a 12-seed can beat a 5-seed. Cover the full landscape, not just the blueblood brands.

### Key Metrics to Reference
- **Team:** KenPom efficiency ratings (AdjO, AdjD, AdjEM), tempo, effective FG%
- **Offense:** turnover rate, offensive rebounding %, assist rate, three-point rate and accuracy
- **Defense:** block%, steal%, opponent eFG%, defensive rebounding %
- **Player:** PER, usage rate, TS%, BPM, win shares per 40 minutes
- **Selection:** NET rankings, Quad 1-4 record, strength of schedule, road/neutral performance

### BSI Differentiator
**Conference depth over brand names.** The Big 12 and SEC produce March Madness upsets because their conference play is a meat grinder — teams battle-tested by February. Cover the teams that emerge from conference play hardened, not just the ones that entered with five-star freshmen. Also: the transfer portal transformed college basketball before it transformed football. Portal movement, its impact on team chemistry, and the one-year rental model are all underserved analytical territory.

### Common Pitfalls
- Treating preseason rankings as meaningful past November
- Ignoring conference tournament seeding implications
- Recency bias after a single signature win or bad loss
- Conflating NBA draft potential with college production (different games)
- Dismissing mid-majors until they win a tournament game

## Cross-Sport Principles

1. **Equal coverage commitment.** No sport is the "offseason filler" for another.
2. **Underdog bias as editorial identity.** The teams and players mainstream media ignores are BSI's territory.
3. **Data + story.** Numbers without narrative is a spreadsheet; narrative without numbers is speculation.
4. **Honesty over access.** Never soften analysis to maintain relationships.
5. **Texas connection acknowledged, not privileged.** The Longhorns, Rangers, Cowboys, Mavs, and Astros get honest coverage—no hometown discount.
