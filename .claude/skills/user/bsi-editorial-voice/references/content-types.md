## Breaking News

**Purpose:** First to inform with accuracy. Speed matters, but never at the cost of truth.

**Structure:**
```
[HEADLINE: What happened in 6-10 words]

[LEDE: Core news in 1-2 sentences—who, what, when, where]

[CONTEXT: 2-3 sentences on why this matters]

[WHAT'S NEXT: Forward-looking 1-2 sentences]

[SOURCE ATTRIBUTION if applicable]
```

**Example lede:**
> Marcus Stroman is headed to the Yankees, per multiple reports—a two-year, $37 million deal that gives New York the innings-eater they've been hunting since Severino's departure.

**Avoid:** Burying the news. Speculation presented as fact. "Developing story" padding.

## Game Recap

**Purpose:** Tell the story of the game, not just report the score. Find the narrative thread.

**Structure:**
```
[OPENING: The defining moment or storyline in 1-2 sentences]

[THE TURN: What shifted the game—could be a play, decision, matchup]

[KEY PERFORMERS: 2-3 players who shaped the outcome, with specific moments]

[CONTEXT: What this means for standings, playoff picture, or season arc]

[LOOKING AHEAD: Next matchup or storyline to watch]
```

**Example opening:**
> Down 14 with six minutes left, the Grizzlies didn't need a miracle—they needed Ja Morant to remember who he is. He did.

**Avoid:** Starting with the final score. Play-by-play chronology. "Both teams played hard" non-analysis.

## Deep Analysis

**Purpose:** Original insight that changes how readers understand a player, team, or trend. This is where BSI earns its reputation.

**Structure:**
```
[THESIS: State the core argument in the opening paragraph]

[EVIDENCE: 3-5 supporting sections, each building the case]
- Mix quantitative (stats, metrics) with qualitative (film observations, context)
- Use specific examples—games, plays, decisions

[COUNTERARGUMENT: Address the strongest objection honestly]

[IMPLICATIONS: What should change based on this analysis—roster decisions, game planning, fan expectations]
```

**Example thesis:**
> The Rangers' pitching development isn't working—and it hasn't been for five years. Their farm system keeps producing arms with stuff, but the major league results have been a parade of underperformance. Here's why their philosophy is broken.

**Key constraint:** Must contain original insight. If ESPN or The Athletic already said it, don't rehash—extend or challenge.

## Player Profile

**Purpose:** Make readers understand a player as both performer and person. Data without humanity is a spreadsheet.

**Structure:**
```
[OPENING: A specific moment or detail that captures who this player is]

[BACKGROUND: Path to current situation—draft, development, key career moments]

[WHAT MAKES THEM DIFFERENT: Specific skills, approach, or traits with evidence]

[THE CHALLENGE: Current obstacle, growth area, or question mark]

[PROJECTION: Where this is heading—grounded in evidence, not optimism]
```

**Example opening:**
> Gunnar Henderson doesn't walk to the plate—he stalks it. Watch the pre-pitch routine: bat waggle, weight shift, eyes locked on the pitcher's release point before the windup begins. This is a hitter who believes the pitcher is the one who should be nervous.

**Avoid:** Wikipedia-style biography. Stats without story. Unearned superlatives.

## Social Posts

**Purpose:** One clear point, delivered with voice. Drive engagement or traffic, but never sacrifice substance for clicks.

### X/Twitter Format

**Breaking news:**
> [NEWS] + [core fact] + [one sentence of context or implication]

**Analysis tease:**
> [Provocative framing of insight] + [1-2 supporting details] + [link or thread indicator]

**Game reaction:**
> [Immediate observation with specificity] + [what it means]

**Example:**
> The Longhorns just lost their third straight game decided by one possession. This isn't bad luck—it's a fourth-quarter execution problem, and Quinn Ewers' decision-making under pressure is the common thread.

**Avoid:** Thread dumps that could be articles. Engagement bait. Vague reactions ("Wow, that was a game").

## Weekly Column

**Purpose:** The signature BSI format—perspective-driven analysis with a consistent voice. Readers come for the take.

**Structure:**
```
[COLD OPEN: Jump straight into the point—no "welcome back" preamble]

[MAIN ARGUMENT: 2-3 paragraphs establishing the thesis with evidence]

[THE NUANCE: Acknowledge complexity, address counterarguments]

[QUICK HITS: 3-5 shorter observations from the week, each 2-3 sentences]

[CLOSING: Forward-looking, preferably with a specific game/player to watch]
```

**Voice note:** Columns are where personality shines. First person is fine. Strong opinions encouraged—but they must be defensible.

## Universal Rules

1. **Headlines should inform, not tease.** Reader should know the story from the headline.
2. **First sentence does the work.** If someone reads only one sentence, they should get value.
3. **Quotes are seasoning, not structure.** Use them to add color, not to avoid doing analysis.
4. **Link to sources.** If citing data or reports, link it.
5. **Timestamp breaking content.** Readers need to know how fresh the information is.
