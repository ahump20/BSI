Colorado Does Not Field a Baseball Team

The University of Colorado Boulder does not sponsor varsity baseball. The program was discontinued in 1980 due to budget constraints and has not been reinstated.

Program History

Colorado had a varsity baseball program from 1895 to 1980, competing for 85 seasons before the program was discontinued. During that time, the Buffaloes had several successful seasons but never reached the College World Series.

Key historical facts:

  • Years Active: 1895-1980 (85 seasons)
  • Conference: Big 8 (baseball)
  • Discontinuation: 1980 due to Title IX compliance and budget constraints
  • Home Field: Potts Field (historical)
  • Notable Fact: Colorado is one of only two current Big 12 members without varsity baseball (Kansas does not field a team either)

There have been periodic discussions about reinstating baseball at Colorado, but the significant costs of facilities, scholarships, and Title IX compliance have prevented the program from returning. Club baseball continues at CU Boulder through student organizations.
