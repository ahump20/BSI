The Origin

I was born August 17, 1995—same day as Davy Crockett—in a Memphis hospital, but my dad put Texas soil under that bed. The soil came from West Columbia, birthplace of the Republic of Texas. The doctor told my parents, "You know you ain't the first to do this—but they've ALL been from Texas."

The news made the local paper in El Campo—my father's hometown, where my grandad Bill founded banks after growing up dirt poor in West Texas and meeting my grandma Helen at Hardin-Simmons following his service in WWII.

"Texas isn't just a place—it's a covenant. Treat people right. Never let anyone stop dreaming beyond the horizon. Root for underdogs."

— The Blaze Philosophy

Why This Exists

ESPN treats college baseball like an afterthought. Fans deserve better. So I built it myself.

After graduating from UT Austin and Full Sail, I spent time at Northwestern Mutual—top 10% nationally—but I got tired of waiting for someone else to fix sports coverage. The platforms that should serve fans instead serve networks. We're here to change that.

The name "Blaze Sports Intel" comes from my dog—a dachshund named Blaze after the first baseball team I played on: the Bartlett Blaze.

The Athletic Background

First time at RB in 7th grade: 70 yards to the house, first play of the season. At Boerne Champion High School, I scored the first touchdown against our big rival Kerrville Tivy. Against Marble Falls, I scored on the first play via screen pass—the play landed on ESPN.

In baseball, I pitched a perfect game. 27 up, 27 down. With the South Texas Sliders, I toured Texas Tech and started that day against the #1 team in the country on Tech's field.

My family has held the same four season tickets to Longhorn football for 40-50 years. I was at the Ricky Williams record-breaking game against A&M.
