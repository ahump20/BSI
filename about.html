From Memphis to Texas: A Journey in Sports Intelligence

Austin Humphrey grew up in Memphis, Tennessee, where sports weren't just games—they were a way of life. From Beale Street blues to the roar of FedExForum, he learned early that championship culture runs deeper than wins and losses. It's about preparation, discipline, and never accepting "good enough."

After moving to Texas and graduating from The University of Texas at Austin, Austin spent years in the trenches—watching coaches struggle with outdated tools, athletes navigate recruiting without real data, and front offices make million-dollar decisions based on gut feelings rather than intelligence.

The breaking point came during a high school playoff game. A coach was flipping through printed scouting reports, trying to adjust strategy mid-game while his opponent had real-time analytics. That's when the mission became clear: build the platform that should have existed all along.

Blaze Sports Intel was born from frustration, forged in competition, and refined through countless conversations with coaches, scouts, and athletes who deserved better. From Boerne, Texas to programs across the Deep South, the platform is helping teams make smarter decisions, faster.

No venture capital pressures. No compromises on data integrity. No inflated promises. Just a championship-caliber platform built by someone who understands what's at stake—because he's lived it.
